Noon Sakinah & Tanween

Step 1: Learn the rules. Step 2: Quiz. Step 3: Play Gold Quest / Kit-style game.

Learn the Rules

Read this like a Quizlet study set before playing.

Izhar (إظهار): Clear pronunciation of Noon Sakinah or Tanween when followed by throat letters (ء ه ع ح غ خ). Example: مِنْ أَهْلِهِ

Idgham (إدغام) with Ghunnah: Merging Noon/Tanween into the next letter with nasalization when followed by ي ن م و. Example: مِنْ نُورٍ

Idgham without Ghunnah: Merging without nasalization when followed by ل ر. Example: مِنْ لَدُنْهُ

Iqlab (إقلاب): Conversion of Noon/Tanween into Meem before ب with concealment. Example: سَمِيعٌ بَصِيرٌ

Ikhfa (إخفاء): Concealment of Noon/Tanween with a light nasal sound before other letters. Example: غَفُورٌ رَحِيمٌ

Meem Sakinah

Learn → Quiz → Game.

Learn the Rules

Ikhfa Shafawi (إخفاء شفوي): Concealment of Meem Sakinah before ب or ف. Example: لَهُمْ فِيهَا

Idgham Shafawi (إدغام شفوي): Merging Meem Sakinah into another Meem. Example: كُنْتُمْ مُّؤْمِنِينَ

Izhar Shafawi (إظهار شفوي): Clear pronunciation of Meem Sakinah before other letters. Example: هُمْ أَصْحَابُ

Madd (Elongation)

Learn → Quiz → Game.

Learn the Rules

Madd Tabee’i / Asli (مد طبيعي): Basic elongation of 2 counts without hamzah or sukoon after the Madd letter. Example: قَالُوا

Madd Jaiz Munfasil (مد جائز منفصل): Madd at the end of a word followed by hamzah at the beginning of the next word. Example: فِي أَنْفُسِكُمْ

Madd Wajib Muttasil (مد واجب متصل): Madd followed by hamzah in the same word. Example: جَاءَكُمْ

Madd Lazim (مد لازم): Madd followed by a permanent sukoon. Example: الصَّاخَّةُ

Madd ‘Arid Lissukoon (مد عارض للسكون): Madd followed by a temporary sukoon when stopping. Example: قَالُواْ

Ghunnah (Nasalization)

Learn → Quiz → Game.

Learn the Rules

Ghunnah (الغنة): Nasal sound of 2 counts on Noon or Meem with shaddah (ّ). Example: إِنَّا أَعْطَيْنَاكَ

Look for Noon/Meem with shaddah in words to identify Ghunnah.

Qalqalah (Echoing Sound)

Learn → Quiz → Game.

Learn the Rules

Qalqalah letters: ق ط ب ج د.

Qalqalah Sughra (صغرى): Echoing inside a word when the letter has sukoon. Example: الْحَقُّ

Qalqalah Kubra (كبرى): Strong echo when stopping on a qalqalah letter with sukoon at the end. Example: وَاللَّهُ أَحَدْ

Rules of Laam (ل)

Learn → Quiz → Game.

Learn the Rules

Laam Shamsiyyah (لام شمسية): Solar Lam, assimilated into the next letter (Laam not pronounced). Example: الشَّمْسُ

Laam Qamariyyah (لام قمرية): Lunar Lam, pronounced clearly. Example: القَمَرُ

Rules of Waqf (Stopping)

Learn → Quiz → Game.

Learn the Rules

Waqf signs: مـ (must stop), لا (do not stop), ج (optional), قلى (better to stop), صلى (better to continue).

Example: الْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ رَبِّ الْعَالَمِينَ ۝ – end of ayah, stop.

Sifaat (Characteristics of Letters)

Learn → Quiz → Game.

Learn the Rules

Tafkheem (تفخيم): Heavy pronunciation, usually for letters in خص ضغط قظ. Example: الصِّرَاطَ

Tarqeeq (ترقيق): Light pronunciation for other letters. Example: السَّبِيلِ

Other sifaat include hams, jahr, shiddah, rikhwah, etc.

Foundational Rules

Learn → Quiz → Game.

Learn the Rules

Hamzat al-Wasl (همزة الوصل): Connecting hamzah that is pronounced at the beginning but dropped in continuous reading. Example: ٱسْمِ رَبِّكَ

Silent Letters (الحروف غير الملفوظة): Letters written but not pronounced in some words.

Connection & Assimilation: Rules about how words connect and letters merge in recitation.
