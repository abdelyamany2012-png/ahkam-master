Lessons

1. Rules of Noon Sakinah (نْ) and Tanween (ــًــٍــٌ)

نْ ــًــٍــٌ
إظهار – Clear إدغام – Merge إقلاب – To Meem إخفاء – Hide

**Izhar (إظهار):** We read the Noon or Tanween clearly, with no merging or hiding.
**Idgham (إدغام):** We merge the Noon/Tanween into the next letter.
With Ghunnah (غنة): ي ن م و
Without Ghunnah: ل ر
**Iqlab (إقلاب):** Noon/Tanween changes to Meem (م) with Ghunnah when followed by ب.
**Ikhfa (إخفاء):** We “hide” the Noon/Tanween and make a soft Ghunnah before many letters.

2. Rules of Meem Sakinah (مْ)

مْ
إخفاء شفوي – Hide with lips إدغام شفوي – Merge with lips إظهار شفوي – Clear with lips

**Ikhfa Shafawi (إخفاء شفوي):** When مْ comes before ب, we hide it slightly with a soft Ghunnah.
**Idgham Shafawi (إدغام شفوي):** When مْ comes before another م, we merge them with Ghunnah.
**Izhar Shafawi (إظهار شفوي):** In other cases, we read مْ clearly with the lips.

3. Rules of Madd (Elongation)

مــد

**Natural Madd (مد طبيعي / مد أصلي):** Simple stretch of 2 counts on letters like ا و ي.

Secondary Madd:
• Madd Jaiz Munfasil (مد جائز منفصل) – word + word, 4–5 counts.
• Madd Wajib Muttasil (مد واجب متصل) – inside one word, 4–5 counts.
• Madd Lazim (مد لازم) – must be long, usually 6 counts.
• Madd ‘Arid Lissukoon (مد عارض للسكون) – at stopping, 2–4–6 counts.

4. Ghunnah (Nasalization)

غــنــة

**Ghunnah (الغنة):** A nasal sound from the nose, usually 2 counts. It appears strongly with letters ن and م, especially in Idgham and Ikhfa.

5. Qalqalah (Echoing Sound)

ق ط ب ج د

Qalqalah happens on certain letters when they are sukoon.
**Qalqalah Sughra (small):** Inside the word, light echo.
**Qalqalah Kubra (big):** At the end when stopping, stronger echo.

6. Rules of Laam (ل)

ل

**Laam Shamsiyyah (لام شمسية):** The ل is “hidden” and the next letter is doubled (like الشمس).
**Laam Qamariyyah (لام قمرية):** The ل is clearly pronounced (like القمر).

7. Rules of Waqf (Stopping)

الــوقــف

Waqf means stopping at a word. Mushafs have signs to show good places to stop or continue. We learn where meaning is complete and avoid stopping in the middle of important meanings.

8. Sifaat (Characteristics of Letters)

ص ض ط ظ ق

**Tafkheem (تفخيم):** Heavy letters, mouth is more open and sound is thick.
**Tarqeeq (ترقيق):** Light letters, sound is soft.
Other sifaat include: hams (breathy), jahr (strong), shiddah (tight), rikhwah (flowing), etc.

9. Additional Foundational Rules

• **Hamzat al-Wasl (همزة الوصل):** A hamzah that is read only when starting, dropped when connecting.
• **Silent Letters (الحروف غير الملفوظة):** Letters written but not pronounced in some words.
• **Connection & Assimilation:** How letters join and sometimes merge when reading smoothly.
